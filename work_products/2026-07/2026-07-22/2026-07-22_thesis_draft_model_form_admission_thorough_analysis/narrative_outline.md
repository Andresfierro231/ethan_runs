# Model-Form Admission Analysis Outline

1. Mdot progress is necessary but not sufficient. Hydraulic/root acceptance does
   not validate TP/TW prediction because thermal source ownership, wall mapping,
   recirculation exchange, and residual closure are separate gates.
2. Residual decomposition precedes fitting. Heater, cooler/jacket, test-section
   source, passive convection, radiation, wall conduction/contact,
   insulation/quartz, storage, recirculation exchange, and unknown residual must
   remain separate from internal Nu.
3. Runtime legality is a hard boundary. CFD mdot, realized wallHeatFlux,
   imposed cooler duty, validation temperatures, holdout temperatures, and
   external-test temperatures are diagnostic evidence only.
4. Common correlations are disabled outside their source envelope. Ordinary pipe
   Nu/fD/K/F6 cannot be admitted on recirculating upcomer evidence.
5. Negative and fail-closed results are useful. They define which evidence is
   missing and prevent overclaiming.
6. Final predictive scores remain zero until exactly one runtime-legal candidate
   is frozen from train/support evidence and scored once on protected rows.
