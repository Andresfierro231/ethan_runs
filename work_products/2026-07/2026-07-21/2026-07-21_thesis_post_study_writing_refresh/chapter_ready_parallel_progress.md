# Parallel External-BC Progress

The external-boundary path is thesis-useful immediately because it separates a
runtime success from a scientific admission. Phase E establishes that the
setup-side external-boundary runtime path executes on the Salt2 training case,
but the resulting thermal residual remains large. Phase F localizes the
dominant residual owner to the heated-incline/TW5 lane, while Phase G and
Phase I keep dictionary completeness and source/sink admissibility from
leaking into a hidden repair.

Phase H and H2 then show why this is not a dead end. The thermal residual is
responsive to external heat-path assumptions: global passive-hA halving moves
TW5 far more than lower-leg-only scaling. That response is not admitted as a
fit. Instead, it narrows the next scientific uncertainty to an independently
sourced external heat-path basis and to missing source/sink or redistribution
physics. The allowed thesis claim is therefore diagnostic localization, not
predictive accuracy, passive-hA admission, or final freeze.
