# Salt Safe-Subset Model Surface Decision

## Primary surface

`Effective UA'(x)` is the preferred Salt modeling surface on the defended branch subset. It keeps the same branch promotion gate as HTC, but it is the most direct branch-scale conductance surface and avoids the extra wall-area normalization step used by HTC.

## Secondary surface

`Effective HTC(x)` remains valuable for interpretation and presentation, but it should stay secondary to `UA'` when the goal is a branch dependency rather than a wall-flux-normalized report quantity.

## Demotions

- `branch-averaged R'_th`: supporting-only
- `R'_th(x)`: diagnostic-only

The reason is not that resistance is meaningless. The reason is that the reciprocal transform adds fragility exactly where support is already thinnest. The safe Salt subset is strong enough to use resistance as a check, but not as the primary fitted surface.
