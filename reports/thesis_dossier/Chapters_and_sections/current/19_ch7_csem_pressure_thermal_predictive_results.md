## Negative Results As Scientific Contributions

The results in this chapter should be read as an admission study, not only as a
search for a final coefficient set. A central scientific contribution is that
the workflow rejects closure labels that would make the reduced model look
simpler while hiding invalid physics, source-envelope violations, or split
leakage. In this sense, the non-admissions are not failed results. They are the
evidence that defines where the model is predictive, diagnostic, blocked, or
score-only.

The first negative contribution is runtime and split gatekeeping. The S0-S3
first-wave package completed four infrastructure studies with `8` consistency
checks, `0` runtime or split leakage failures, and `0` fit-enabled final rows.
That result is useful because it proves that the predictive-model path can be
audited before any score is reported. It does not create final predictive
accuracy.

The second contribution is the recirculation guard. The S4 upcomer package
reviewed `90` ordinary single-stream candidates, found `45` reverse-flow or
exchange diagnostic rows, and admitted `0` ordinary upcomer `Nu/f_D/K` rows
and `0` exchange-cell coefficients. The thesis result is therefore a validity
boundary: current recirculating upcomer evidence is enough to disable ordinary
pipe closure language, but not enough to fit an exchange-cell model. S9 then
turns the missing exchange evidence into a specific next-study contract rather
than a vague recirculation caveat.

The updated S12 result extends that same negative-evidence logic to thermal
shape. It names `S12-HIAX1` as the best current heated-incline/upcomer
exchange-shape owner for the TW5/TW6 residual, while still releasing `0`
admitted candidates and `0` S11-ready candidates. This lets the thesis state
what the next physical candidate is without claiming that the candidate has
passed the train-only solve, attribution, freeze, validation, holdout, or
external-test sequence.

The third contribution is pressure and F6 non-admission. The current
pressure-corner synthesis reviewed `83` same-QOI rows and `3` pressure-corner
rows while admitting `0` component-K, F6, clipped-K, or hidden-multiplier
closures. S10 repeats the same discipline at the low-recirculation/F6 frontier
and still releases `0` S11-ready pressure candidates. This replaces tempting
pressure fixes with a stricter sequence: pressure basis, component isolation,
recirculation status, same-QOI uncertainty, source/property labels, and
comparison to the current `F3_shah_apparent` baseline.

The fourth contribution is source/property and split release discipline. The
S5 release package reviewed `1110` source/property-enforced candidate rows,
found `0` missing required labels, and still released `0` rows for fitting or
model selection. Label completeness is therefore not treated as fit
permission. It is a prerequisite for a later candidate-specific release.

The fifth contribution is the blocked frozen scorecard. S6 publishes the
scorecard shell with `16` split-role rows, `1` frozen-candidate placeholder,
`0` fit/model-selection rows, and `0` final score values. The empty scorecard
is a result because it makes the missing evidence explicit: no held-out,
external, or future row is scored until a runtime-legal candidate is
predeclared and frozen.

The sixth contribution is sensor-map runtime discipline. The S7 sensor-map
contract separates TP/TW score interpretation from model construction. It maps
or bounds the probe evidence while allowing `0` runtime temperature inputs and
`0` fit targets from TP/TW data. Temperature residuals can therefore support
post-solve diagnostics, but they do not become hidden calibration anchors.

The seventh contribution is wall/test-section falsification. S8 reviews the
current setup-only wall/test-section and axial-mixing candidate families and
finds `0` admitted candidates and `0` S11-ready candidates. This converts a
broad blocker into a sharper research boundary: the next useful work is a new
physical candidate, production upcomer exchange QOIs, or nonrecirculating
pressure-anchor evidence, not another unguarded selector sweep over the same
families.

Together, these negative results strengthen the thesis claim. The reduced
modeling workflow is not a route to any available coefficient that improves a
single metric. It is a provenance-controlled admission process that decides
which terms are runtime-legal, which terms are diagnostic, which targets are
score-only, and which closures require more evidence before prediction.

## Chapter-Ready Wording

The current results support a strong but bounded thesis claim. The CFD evidence
shows that heat and hydraulic burdens are spatially structured rather than
uniform loop corrections. Junction/stub heat ledgers show that local thermal
ownership matters. Two-tap pressure evidence shows that local pressure

straight/developing references, recirculation status, and uncertainty before a

gets worse.

The predictive path remains open. The thesis can report the M0-M6 ladder and
the final scorecard shell, but it should not invent final M6 scores. That is a
feature of the admission workflow, not a gap to hide. The current result is a
vetted route from CFD evidence to a future frozen prediction, with the blocked
closures named rather than absorbed into a global multiplier.
