## Scope

Time-resolved postprocessor analysis across all Ethan CFD runs (jadyn + modern):
- Parse OpenFOAM `postProcessing` scalar time series and plot values vs time,
  grouped by quantity family (mdot legs, temperature probes, wall-temperature
  probes, total heat Q).
- Companion last-window (~5 min / 300 s of simulated time) plot per group with a
  linear trendline, the fitted equation in the legend, and R².
- Report RMSE and variance of the oscillation (about mean and about trend),
  quantify oscillations, and give a steady-state verdict.
- Compare the mean's uncertainty via CLT / 1-over-sqrt(t): standard error of the
  mean (naive 1/sqrt(N) and autocorrelation-corrected), plus a SEM-vs-averaging-
  time convergence plot with a 1/sqrt(t) reference.
- Reusable scripts, rigorous documentation, provenance, data disclosure.

## Findings

- mdot + temperatures are steady in nearly all runs (stationary, gently oscillating).
- `total_Q` is the NET heat residual (~0.1 W vs hundreds-of-W loop scale); its
  "not steady" verdicts are the residual's relative noisiness, not thermal runaway.
  Fixed a real bug where near-zero-mean series were falsely flagged "drifting" —
  verdict now uses a scale-free drift_ratio when the mean is near zero.
- The only not-steady mdot rows are salt3 corrected-Q perturbations (hi5q/hi10q),
  mass flow still moving — consistent with the corrected-Salt gate (AGENT-237,
  "too short / needs extended continuation").
