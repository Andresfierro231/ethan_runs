## Inferred Interpretation

The user-stated blocker that `enthalpy_change_W` was entirely unavailable is
partly superseded by AGENT-203 and the July 8 heat-ledger update. The remaining
paper-grade blocker is narrower and more important: the present enthalpy
residuals are based on coarse span endpoints, not a complete physical
segment-interface registry aligned to every heat-source/sink region. A new
follow-up package should therefore preserve the July 8 foundation as provenance
and rebuild the enthalpy/residual layer at physical segment interfaces.

## Blockers

- Physical segment inlet/outlet surfaces must be identified or generated for
  heater, cooler, passive-wall, test-section, and junction-adjacent regions.
- Junction losses cannot be converted into enthalpy-flow residuals until the
  flow entering and leaving each junction group is bracketed by defensible
  interfaces.
- Upcomer residuals require recirculation-aware interpretation and should
  remain validation-only unless the method can handle reversing flow without
  hiding multi-stream energy transport.
- A complete resistance network still depends on explicit internal-convection,
  wall-conduction, external-convection, and radiation data. Radiation must only
  become a heat term if OpenFOAM exposes `qr`.
- Mesh uncertainty and corrected-Salt perturbation admission remain separate
  work in progress and should not be silently folded into this follow-up.
